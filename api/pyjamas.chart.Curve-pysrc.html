 230      """ 
 231      * Separates points on this curve into bins associated with 
 232      * successive vertical (or horizontal) bands across the entire 
 233      * decorated chart. 
 234      * <p> 
 235      * 
 236      * Because busy charts typically distribute points evenly 
 237      * across the chart, by jumping to the appropriate band's 
 238      * list, we can (usually) greatly accelerate worst case mouse 
 239      * hit testing. And because the bin organizing step only 
 240      * requires a single pass over all the points (and less than a 
 241      * two int memory overhead per point) it should almost always 
 242      * be a "good deal" performance-wise (compared to a simple 
 243      * full point-list scan with every hit test).<p> 
 244      * 
 245      * Points are placed into bins based on the (pixel) position 
 246      * of the x (or, with horizontal bands, y) at the center of 
 247      * the rendered symbol.  We choose bin size to guarantee that 
 248      * bins are at least as wide (or high, for horizontally banded 
 249      * hit testing) as the rendered symbols on this curve. This 
 250      * simplifies hit testing, since bins are big enough to assure 
 251      * that a single symbol straddles at most two adjacent bands. 
 252      * Exploits fact that all symbols on the same curve have the 
 253      * same size, and that curves with many points on them tend to 
 254      * have smaller sized symbols. 
 255      * 
 256      * <p> 
 257      * 
 258      * Note that, for bin placement purposes, pie slices are 
 259      * considered to have a "center" equal to the center of the 
 260      * pie that contains them, and to have a width and height 
 261      * equal to the diameter of the pie containing the slice 
 262      * (the "worst-case slice"). 
 263      * 
 264      * <p> 
 265      * 
 266      * Also note that a symbol whose center is in the right side 
 267      * of a vertical band may overlap into the following band, and 
 268      * one in the left side may overlap the preceding band. Thus 
 269      * during hit testing, we must check not only the lists of 
 270      * points in the bands "touched" by the mouse-cursor-centered 
 271      * brush, but also 1) the band to the immediate left of the 
 272      * leftmost touched band, whenever a left-side sub-band of that band 
 273      * is touched by the brush and 2) the band to the immediate 
 274      * right of the rightmost touched band, whenever a right-side 
 275      * sub-band of that band is touched. The thickness of these left 
 276      * and right side sub-bands equals half the symbol width. 
 277      * Expanding the brush a half-symbol width on either edge 
 278      * is the easiest way to apply these rules. Exactly 
 279      * analogous statements apply to horizontal bands.  <p> 
 280      * 
 281      * A minimum band size is enforced to prevent the number of 
 282      * bands from growing too large with small symbols. Each 
 283      * symbol type defines if vertical or horizontal banding is 
 284      * more appropriate, or if brush shape should determine 
 285      * banding strategy (as of this writing, only horizontal bar 
 286      * symbol types require horizontal hit-test bands).  Exploits the 
 287      * fact that all symbols have a fixed size for at least one of 
 288      * their dimensions (for example, vertical bars have variable 
 289      * height but fixed width).<p>
